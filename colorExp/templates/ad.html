Computational Modeling of Human and Machine Intelligence

Call for participants

The Human Optimization Lab at the University of Colorado Boulder (CU Boulder) is looking for online participants for a brief experiment on how people reason about shapes.

In this experiment, you will make judgments about the names of shapes. On each trial, you will see a shape and two words. Your job is to click on the word you believe corresponds best to the given shape. The experiment takes 15 minutes on average.

The requirements are:

  • You are at least 18 years old,
  • You have normal vision or wear corrective lenses
  • You are not colorblind, and
  • You are a fluent English speaker.

Note: the experiment will only work in recent browsers, and may have difficulty loading on slower connections.

This task can only be completed once. If you have already completed this task before the system will not allow you to run again. If this looks familiar please release the hit so someone else can participate.
