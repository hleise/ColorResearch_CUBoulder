All trials Completed!

The purpose of this experiment is to determine if providing additional visual information (which we refer to as decisional support) can be used to improve an individual’s ability to categorize images. In this experiment, the type of decisional support was varied in order to understand what factors are important for accurate performance. For example, if additional information is presented in the form of example images from a category, it is important that the example images be selected carefully. For instance, images could be selected in a way that takes into account how humans perceive similarity between objects. Your data will help us determine the factors—such as the method of image selection—that impact categorization performance. Our hypothesis is that decisional support can improve categorization performance.

Thank you for assisting with our research. Your results will be combined with around 2000 other participants to help advance the state of scientific knowledge. If you would like to learn more, please contact Brett Roads at brett.roads@colorado.edu.
